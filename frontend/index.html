Frequently Asked Questions

TTMM (Table Tennis Match Matcher) helps you find a table tennis partner based on your skill level, available time, and preferred location. No more spamming group chats — just create your profile and find a match!

Click "My Profile" in the navigation bar, fill in your name, skill level, available time slots, preferred places, and contact info. Click "Save Profile" and you're ready to go!

Go to the "Players" page to see all registered players. You can switch between card and table view using the toggle buttons. Click "Send Request" on anyone you'd like to play with.

💡 Pro tips: Use the Filters panel to narrow down players by skill, place, or time. Even better — once you have a profile, just click the "✨ Find Matches" button to instantly see players that match your level, place, and schedule!

Contact info is never shown publicly. When you send a request, both parties must approve it. Once both approve, you can click "View Contacts" to see each other's contact details. If either party declines, contacts stay hidden.

Selecting "Anywhere" means you're flexible about the playing location. You can also pick specific default places (like "4th dorm" or "Sport complex") or add a custom place that isn't in the default list.

Your profile is tied to your Google account. Sign in with Google to access your profile automatically. If you previously had a profile without authentication, you may need to create a new one after signing in.

Yes! Go to "My Profile" at any time to update your information. You must be signed in with Google to edit your profile. Changes are saved immediately when you click the save button, and you'll be redirected to the Players page to see your updated profile.

Currently, TTMM supports signing in with your Google account for a quick and secure experience. We're planning to add more sign-in options in the future, such as Yandex and Telegram, so stay tuned!

Signing in with Google ensures your profile is secure and persistent across devices. It also prevents spam and keeps contact info private — only mutually approved matches can see each other's contact details. We never share your Google data publicly — your email is only used to link your account.

Try pressing Ctrl+Shift+R (or Cmd+Shift+R on Mac) to force a hard refresh of the page. This clears the browser cache and loads the latest version of the app. If the problem persists, check the browser console (F12) for error messages and report them.
